==========================================================
  GOOGLE SEARCH CONSOLE SETUP  (get Stef's site into Google)
  Site: https://www.callmyfriendstef.com
==========================================================

Google Search Console (free) is how you tell Google your site
exists, submit your sitemap, and watch which searches bring
people in. Do this once the site is live on your real domain.

Two things happen here:
  A) Verify you own the site
  B) Submit the sitemap
Then Google starts crawling and ranking your pages.

----------------------------------------------------------
PART A — CREATE THE ACCOUNT & VERIFY
----------------------------------------------------------

1. Go to https://search.google.com/search-console and sign in
   with the Google account you want to manage this from
   (using the same Google account as the Business Profile is
   handy but not required).

2. Click "Add property." You'll see two choices:

   >>> RECOMMENDED (easiest for us): "URL prefix"
       - Type exactly:  https://www.callmyfriendstef.com
       - Click Continue.
       - Choose the "HTML tag" verification method.
       - Google shows a line that looks like:
           <meta name="google-site-verification" content="XXXXXXXX" />
       - COPY that whole line and send it to Kyle/Claude.
         We'll paste it into the site's <head> and push it
         live (takes 1 minute).
       - Once it's live, come back and click "Verify."

   Alternative (no code needed, but needs domain login):
   >>> "Domain" property
       - Type:  callmyfriendstef.com
       - Google gives you a TXT record to add at your domain
         registrar (wherever the domain is registered) or in
         Netlify's DNS settings.
       - Add the TXT record, wait a few minutes, click Verify.
       - This one verifies the whole domain (www + non-www).

   (Netlify note: if Netlify manages your DNS, you can also add
   the TXT record under Netlify > Domains > DNS records.)

----------------------------------------------------------
PART B — SUBMIT THE SITEMAP
----------------------------------------------------------

3. Once verified, in the left menu click "Sitemaps."

4. Under "Add a new sitemap," type:
        sitemap.xml
   (the box already shows https://www.callmyfriendstef.com/ —
    you only type "sitemap.xml")  then click Submit.

5. It should say "Success." Google now knows about all pages:
        /                              (home)
        /kennewick-mortgage-broker.html
        /portland-mortgage-broker.html
        /texas-mortgage-broker.html

----------------------------------------------------------
PART C — GIVE IT A NUDGE (optional but helpful)
----------------------------------------------------------

6. Use the search bar at the very top ("Inspect any URL").
   Paste https://www.callmyfriendstef.com/ and click
   "Request Indexing." Repeat for each of the 3 location pages.
   This asks Google to crawl them sooner.

----------------------------------------------------------
WHAT TO EXPECT
----------------------------------------------------------
- Indexing takes a few days to a couple weeks. Be patient.
- After a week or two, "Performance" shows the search terms
  people used to find the site and where you rank.
- "Pages" (Indexing) shows which pages Google has indexed and
  flags any problems.

----------------------------------------------------------
TWO QUICK EXTRAS
----------------------------------------------------------
- TEST RICH RESULTS: paste any page into
  https://search.google.com/test/rich-results to preview the
  star rating, FAQ, and breadcrumbs Google can show.

- BING (optional, 5 min): https://www.bing.com/webmasters
  lets you "Import from Google Search Console" in one click.
  Bing also feeds some AI assistants (Copilot), so it's worth it.
