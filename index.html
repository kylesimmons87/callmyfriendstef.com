Good to Know

Frequently asked questions

What's the difference between pre-qualified and pre-approved?

Pre-qualification is a quick, informal estimate of what you might be able to borrow. Pre-approval is stronger: I verify your income, assets, and credit, so you get a documented amount to shop with — and sellers take your offer more seriously. I always recommend getting pre-approved before you house-hunt.

Can I buy a home with as little as 3% down?

Often, yes. Several conventional programs allow as little as 3% down, FHA goes as low as 3.5%, and VA and USDA loans can offer 0% down for those who qualify — plus there are down-payment assistance programs. The right fit depends on your situation, which is exactly what I help you figure out.

How much income do I need to buy a home?

There's no single number — it depends on the price, your down payment, your other monthly debts, and current rates. Lenders look at your debt-to-income ratio, not income alone. Try my affordability calculator for a ballpark, then we'll dial in a real number together.

What credit score do I need to buy a home?

It varies by loan program — some allow lower scores than people expect, with options across a wide credit range. A higher score usually means better terms, but don't count yourself out. If your credit needs work, I can point you in the right direction before you apply.

How long does it take to close on a mortgage?

Most purchase loans close in about 30 days, though it can be faster or slower depending on the loan type, appraisal, and how quickly documents come together. I keep things moving and communicate every step so there are no surprises.

What are closing costs, and how much should I expect?

Closing costs are the fees to finalize your loan — appraisal, title, lender fees, and more — and typically run around 2–5% of the loan amount. Some can be negotiated, rolled in, or covered by seller credits. I'll walk you through a clear estimate up front.

How do interest rates affect my monthly payment?

Even a small rate change moves your payment, since interest is charged on your whole balance over many years. See exactly how much with my payment calculator. As a broker, I shop multiple lenders to find you a competitive rate.

What types of home loans do you offer?

As an independent broker I offer conventional, FHA, VA, USDA, and jumbo loans, plus refinancing and cash-out options — shopping multiple lenders to find the best rate and fit for you.

Do you offer bank statement, ITIN, or DSCR loans?

Yes. I offer bank statement loans (qualify with bank statements instead of tax returns — ideal for the self-employed), ITIN loans for borrowers without a Social Security number, and DSCR loans for real estate investors that qualify on the property's rental income. These non-QM programs are built for borrowers who don't fit the traditional box.

How is a mortgage broker different from a bank?

Instead of a single bank's products, I work with many lenders and compare their rates and programs on your behalf — so you get options, not just one institution's answer.
